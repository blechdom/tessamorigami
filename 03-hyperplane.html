METHOD NO METAPHOR REQUIRED

The contacts
are the instrument.

The plane is written as p(u,v) = c + uU + vV, where every point has X, Y, Z, and W coordinates. Eight inequalities—positive and negative bounds for each of the four axes—clip its initially open surface to the interior of the tesseract.

The remaining polygon is the complete plane section. Its corners are places where at least two boundary constraints meet. The wireframe, section, and dots all pass through the same 4D-to-3D-to-screen projection; they are three views of one calculation.

Each corner drives one quiet oscillator. Position in four dimensions determines pitch, projected horizontal position determines stereo placement, and W depth affects tone color. When the section changes topology, the number of simultaneous voices changes with it.
