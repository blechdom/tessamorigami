WHY THIS ONE

One geometry.
Three readings.

This study treats the visible surface as data, not decoration. The moving plane cuts the deformed field; the bright contour and its dots are computed from the same intersections that drive the sound. Dragging moves a local deformation through the lattice.

The pitch mapping changes which property becomes easiest to hear. View depth makes rotation audible, field position preserves a stable reference while the surface moves, and local strain exposes where the lattice is being stretched. Horizontal projection always controls stereo position.
