THE SOUND

playhead.

Every audible voice is attached to a real node. The most strained and fastest-moving nodes rise out of the field; their horizontal positions set stereo placement and their heights set pitch. Floor impacts create short plucks at the point of contact.

Gravity, stiffness, damping, and wind change both the geometry and the sound because there is only one underlying simulation. Pull slowly to expose tension. Flick a node to send a visible and audible wave across the lattice.
